Apple just answered its biggest AI question.





By choosing someone else's answer.


The new Siri is here.
AI-powered. Context-aware.
Cross-app integration.

But here's the part most people are glossing over.
Apple didn't build the model.

They partnered with Google.
Gemini is running under the hood.
The biggest tech company on the planet looked at the AI race and decided:

We're not building this ourselves.

That's not weakness. That's strategy.

I think about this a lot as someone who builds AI products.
The instinct is always to build everything from scratch.
Own the stack. Control the model. Do it yourself.

But the smartest builders know when to build and when to integrate.

Apple kept what they're best at:
Hardware. Ecosystem. Privacy (Private Cloud Compute).
And they brought in the best available model for the intelligence layer.

This is the "build vs. buy" decision playing out at the highest level.
And it applies to every scale.

Whether you're a solo developer or a Fortune 10 company, the question is the same:
Where does your real advantage live?

• Apple's advantage is ecosystem and distribution, not model training
• Google's advantage is model capability, not consumer hardware
• Your advantage as a builder is speed and execution, not reinventing infrastructure

The companies winning in AI right now aren't the ones building everything.
They're the ones assembling the right pieces.

I've seen this in my own work.
When I started building AI products, I wanted to own every layer.
Custom models. Custom pipelines. Custom everything.

It slowed me down.

The shift happened when I started asking a different question.
Not "can I build this?"
But "should I build this?"

If someone else has already solved it better, use it.
Spend your energy where only you can add value.

Apple just validated this at a trillion-dollar scale.

Build where you're strong.
Integrate where you're not.

That's not compromise.
That's clarity.
