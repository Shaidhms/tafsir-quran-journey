The Palm Fiber - When Wealth and Power Can't Save You

Makki Surah · 5 Verses · 23 Words

Al-Masad: A Visual Journey

The only surah that names a specific enemy. A prophecy that came true.

1
تَبَّتْ يَدَا أَبِي لَهَبٍ وَتَبَّ
Tabbat yada Abi Lahabin wa tabb
Abu Lahab was the Prophet's own uncle. He used his hands, his power, his influence to fight Islam at every turn. Allah says: those hands are RUINED. And he himself is ruined. Everything he did to stop the truth? It failed. Completely.
2
مَا أَغْنَىٰ عَنْهُ مَالُهُ وَمَا كَسَبَ
Ma aghna 'anhu maluhu wa ma kasab
He was rich. He was powerful. He had status. But none of it helped him. Not his money, not what he earned, not his connections. When you stand against the truth, no amount of wealth or power can protect you from the consequences.
3
سَيَصْلَىٰ نَارًا ذَاتَ لَهَبٍ
Sayasla naran dhata lahab
His nickname was "Abu Lahab" meaning "Father of Flame" because of his handsome, glowing face. Allah turns this into a devastating wordplay: the "Father of Flame" will enter a fire OF flame. The very thing he was known for becomes his punishment.
4
وَامْرَأَتُهُ حَمَّالَةَ الْحَطَبِ
Wamra'atuhu hammalatal-hatab
His wife Umm Jamil was just as bad. She would literally place thorny branches on the path where the Prophet walked. She also "carried firewood" as in she spread gossip and fuel for conflict. She was his partner in fighting the truth.
5
فِي جِيدِهَا حَبْلٌ مِّن مَّسَدٍ
Fi jidiha hablun min masad
The final image: around her neck, instead of the expensive necklaces she wore in this life, will be a rough rope of twisted palm fiber. The surah is NAMED after this rope. It's the ultimate reversal: luxury replaced by the roughest, most humiliating material.

The Story Behind This Surah

Abu Lahab (real name: Abdul-Uzza ibn Abdul-Muttalib) was the Prophet Muhammad's own uncle. When the Prophet was commanded to warn his closest relatives, he climbed Mount Safa and called out to the tribes of Quraysh one by one.

He asked them: "If I told you there was an army behind this mountain about to attack you, would you believe me?" They all said: "Yes, we have never known you to lie." Then he said: "I am warning you of a severe punishment."

Abu Lahab's response: "May you perish! Is this why you gathered us?"

Allah immediately responded with this surah, turning Abu Lahab's own curse back on him: "Tabbat yada Abi Lahab" - May the hands of Abu Lahab perish.

Source: Sahih al-Bukhari 4971

This surah is a living prophecy. It was revealed when Abu Lahab was alive and powerful. It declared he would never accept Islam and would be destroyed. Abu Lahab had every opportunity to disprove this surah by simply saying "I accept Islam" - even insincerely. But he never did. He died as a disbeliever, exactly as the Quran stated he would. This is one of the boldest prophecies in the Quran.

His wife Umm Jamil (Arwa bint Harb) was the sister of Abu Sufyan. She was known for placing thorny branches on the Prophet's path at night and spreading gossip to turn people against him.

When Umm Jamil heard this surah was revealed about her, she came to the Prophet while he was sitting with Abu Bakr, carrying a stone. Allah blinded her from seeing the Prophet. She asked Abu Bakr: "Where is your companion? I've heard he has satirized me." Abu Bakr said nothing, and she left. Abu Bakr said: "O Messenger of Allah, didn't she see you?" He replied: "Allah took away her sight from me."

Source: Tafsir Ibn Kathir

Verse by Verse - With Word-by-Word Arabic
1
تَبَّتْ يَدَا أَبِي لَهَبٍ وَتَبَّ
Tabbat yada Abi Lahabin wa tabb
May the hands of Abu Lahab be ruined, and ruined is he.

Word by Word (read right to left)

تَبَّتْtabbatPerished / Ruined
يَدَاyadahands (of)
أَبِي لَهَبٍAbi LahabAbu Lahab
وَتَبَّwa tabband ruined is he
Simple Explanation

"Tabbat" (perished) appears twice: first for his hands (his actions, his efforts against Islam), and then for himself entirely (wa tabb). The "hands" represent all his scheming and plotting. "Abu Lahab" means "Father of Flame" - he was given this name because of his radiant, handsome face. But Allah uses his own name against him. This is the only surah where a specific enemy of Islam is named by name in the Quran.

2
مَا أَغْنَىٰ عَنْهُ مَالُهُ وَمَا كَسَبَ
Ma aghna 'anhu maluhu wa ma kasab
His wealth will not avail him or that which he gained.

Word by Word

مَاmaNot / What
أَغْنَىٰaghnaavailed / benefited
عَنْهُ'anhuhim
مَالُهُmaluhuhis wealth
وَمَا كَسَبَwa ma kasaband what he earned
Simple Explanation

"Ma aghna" - his wealth did NOT make him independent of Allah's decree. "Mal" (wealth) and "ma kasab" (what he earned) cover everything: his money, his children (some scholars say this refers to his children whom he was proud of), his status, his reputation. None of it could protect him. This is a universal warning: no amount of worldly success can shield you from the consequences of fighting the truth.

3
سَيَصْلَىٰ نَارًا ذَاتَ لَهَبٍ
Sayasla naran dhata lahab
He will burn in a Fire of blazing flame.

Word by Word

سَيَصْلَىٰsa-yaslaHe will enter/burn
نَارًاnarana fire
ذَاتَdhatapossessing / of
لَهَبٍlahabflame
Simple Explanation

"Sa" (he will) - future tense, a definite promise. "Yasla" - to enter and burn in fire. And the fire is described as "dhata lahab" (possessing flame/blazing). Here's the devastating wordplay: Abu LAHAB (Father of Flame) will enter a fire of LAHAB (flame). His very name, once a compliment about his handsome face, becomes a description of his punishment. This is also a prophecy - it declares with certainty that he will never accept Islam.

4
وَامْرَأَتُهُ حَمَّالَةَ الْحَطَبِ
Wamra'atuhu hammalatal-hatab
And his wife, the carrier of firewood.

Word by Word

وَامْرَأَتُهُwamra'atuhuAnd his wife
حَمَّالَةَhammalatacarrier (of)
الْحَطَبِal-hatabthe firewood
Simple Explanation

"Hammalat al-hatab" (carrier of firewood) has multiple layers of meaning. Literally: she used to place thorny branches on the path where the Prophet walked at night. Figuratively: she "carried firewood" by spreading gossip, slander, and fuel for conflict between people. In the Hereafter: she will carry firewood for the fire that she and her husband will enter. The word "hammalah" is an intensive form - she didn't just carry occasionally, she was a habitual, enthusiastic carrier.

5
فِي جِيدِهَا حَبْلٌ مِّن مَّسَدٍ
Fi jidiha hablun min masad
Around her neck is a rope of palm fiber.

Word by Word

فِيfiIn / Around
جِيدِهَاjidihaher neck
حَبْلٌhabluna rope
مِّن مَّسَدٍmin masadof palm fiber
Simple Explanation

The word "jid" is actually a poetic, elegant word for neck - usually used to describe a beautiful neck adorned with jewelry. The irony is sharp: Allah uses a word associated with beauty and elegance, but what adorns her neck is not gold or pearls - it's "hablun min masad" - a rope made of rough, twisted palm fiber. She who wore expensive necklaces in this life will wear the roughest rope in the next. Al-Masad (palm fiber) gives this surah its name.

Arabic Wordplay: The Name Becomes the Punishment

One of the most powerful examples of Arabic wordplay in the Quran:

أَبُو لَهَبٍ
Abu Lahab (Father of Flame)
→
نَارًا ذَاتَ لَهَبٍ
A fire of LAHAB (flame)

His name was a compliment about his radiant face. The Quran turns it into a description of his eternal punishment.

Grammar Tip: The Intensive Form (fa''al)

"Hammalah" (carrier) uses the fa''alah pattern, which indicates someone who does something excessively and habitually.

حَمَّالَة
Hammalah
"Habitual carrier" (intensive)
حَامِلَة
Hamilah
"One who carries" (normal)

The intensive form was used to show she didn't just carry firewood once - it was her regular, enthusiastic activity to harm the Prophet and spread conflict.

Key Lessons from Al-Masad
  • ◆
    Family ties don't guarantee guidance.

    Abu Lahab was the Prophet's own uncle. Being close to the truth doesn't mean you'll accept it. Faith is a personal choice.

  • ◆
    Wealth and power are meaningless without faith.

    Abu Lahab had money, status, and influence. None of it could save him. Don't let worldly success make you arrogant against the truth.

  • ◆
    This surah is a living prophecy.

    It was revealed while Abu Lahab was alive. He could have "disproved" it by accepting Islam. He never did. This is evidence of the Quran's divine origin.

  • ◆
    Your partner matters.

    Abu Lahab and his wife were partners in fighting the truth. Choose a spouse who supports your journey toward good, not away from it.

  • ◆
    Gossip is spiritual firewood.

    "Carrying firewood" is a metaphor for spreading conflict and gossip. Words can fuel destruction just like physical fire.
