The Story Behind This Surah

Surah Al-Falaq is one of the Mu'awwidhatayn - the two surahs of seeking refuge. It is paired with Surah An-Nas (114). Together, they form a complete shield: Al-Falaq protects from external evils, and An-Nas protects from internal evils (whispers in the heart).

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) was afflicted by magic cast by Labid ibn al-A'sam, who tied knots and blew on them. Jibreel (angel Gabriel) came to the Prophet with Surah Al-Falaq and Surah An-Nas. As each verse was recited, a knot was untied, until the Prophet was completely freed from the spell.

Source: Sahih al-Bukhari 5765

The Prophet (peace be upon him) would recite Al-Falaq and An-Nas every night before sleeping. He would blow into his cupped hands and then wipe them over his body, starting with his head and face, doing this three times.

Uqbah ibn Amir reported that the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) said: "Do you not see that there have been verses revealed to me tonight the like of which has never been seen? 'Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of daybreak' and 'Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind.'"

Source: Sahih Muslim 814

This surah identifies four specific evils you seek protection from: the evil of creation in general, the evil of darkness, the evil of sorcery (blowing on knots), and the evil of envy. Each one is a real, tangible threat that Allah teaches you to guard against with His words.

The word "Al-Falaq" means the splitting of dawn - that moment when light cracks through the darkness. It is a powerful image: just as Allah splits the night with light every single morning, He can split apart any darkness, any evil, any harm that surrounds you.

Key Lessons from Al-Falaq
  • ◆
    Evil exists, but Allah's protection is greater.

    The surah acknowledges real threats but immediately teaches you the remedy: turning to Allah. His protection overpowers every evil.

  • ◆
    Darkness is temporary - dawn always comes.

    The name "Al-Falaq" itself is a promise. No matter how dark it gets, light will break through. Allah is the Lord of that breakthrough.

  • ◆
    Magic is real but powerless against Allah's words.

    The Prophet himself was affected by magic, and the cure was these very verses. If the Quran is your shield, no spell can penetrate it.

  • ◆
    Envy destroys the envier more than the envied.

    Envy is listed alongside sorcery and darkness as a source of evil. Guard your heart from it, and seek protection from those who carry it.

  • ◆
    Daily recitation is your spiritual shield.

    The Prophet recited Al-Falaq and An-Nas every night. Make it your habit - these words are your armor against the unseen.
