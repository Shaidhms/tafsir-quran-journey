The Story Behind This Surah

Surah Al-Qadr announces one of the most magnificent events in human history: the revelation of the Quran. Allah chose a specific night to begin sending down His final message to mankind, and that night is called Laylatul Qadr (the Night of Power, Decree, or Destiny).

This night falls in the last ten nights of Ramadan. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Search for Laylatul Qadr in the odd nights of the last ten nights of Ramadan." (Sahih al-Bukhari). Many scholars consider the 27th night as the most likely, though it could be any of the odd nights.

Ibn Kathir narrates that the Prophet (peace be upon him) was shown the lifespans of previous nations, and he felt that the lifespan of his Ummah was too short to compete with them in good deeds. So Allah granted this Ummah Laylatul Qadr, a single night whose worship equals more than 1,000 months (83+ years). This was an exclusive gift to the Muslim Ummah.

Source: Tafsir Ibn Kathir, Tafsir As-Sa'di

The word "Qadr" carries multiple meanings: power, decree, honor, and measurement. On this night, the decrees for the coming year are handed over from the Preserved Tablet (Al-Lawh Al-Mahfuz) to the angels who will carry them out. It is a night of immense honor, where worship is multiplied beyond measure.

As-Sa'di explains that this surah highlights three extraordinary aspects of Laylatul Qadr: its superiority over a thousand months, the descent of angels with mercy and blessings, and the complete peace that envelops the earth from sunset to dawn.

Verse by Verse - With Word-by-Word Arabic

Each verse is broken down word by word. Learn the Arabic gradually by connecting each word to its meaning.

1
إِنَّا أَنزَلْنَاهُ فِي لَيْلَةِ الْقَدْرِ
Inna anzalnahu fi laylatil-qadr
Indeed, We sent it down during the Night of Power.

Word by Word (right to left)

فِيfiIn / during
لَيْلَةِlaylatiNight of
الْقَدْرِal-qadrThe Power / Decree
Simple Explanation

Allah uses the royal "We" (inna) to emphasize the majesty of the event. "It" (hu) refers to the Quran, though the Quran is not mentioned by name, showing how well-known it already was. The Quran was sent down in its entirety from the Preserved Tablet (Al-Lawh Al-Mahfuz) to the lowest heaven (Bayt Al-'Izzah) on this night, and then revealed gradually to the Prophet over 23 years through Jibreel (peace be upon him). Ibn Kathir and Ibn Abbas both confirm this understanding.

2
وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ
Wa ma adraka ma laylatul-qadr
And what can make you know what the Night of Power is?

Word by Word (right to left)

وَمَاwa maAnd what
أَدْرَاكَadrakaCan make you know
مَاmaWhat
لَيْلَةُlaylatuNight of
الْقَدْرِal-qadrThe Power / Decree
Simple Explanation

This is a rhetorical question used in the Quran to build awe and anticipation. Whenever Allah says "wa ma adraka" (and what will make you know), it means the reality of this thing is beyond human comprehension. As-Sa'di says this phrase is used to magnify and exalt the subject, indicating that this night is so extraordinary that no human mind can fully grasp its greatness. Allah is about to answer His own question in the next verse.

3
لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌ مِّنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍ
Laylatul-qadri khayrum min alfi shahr
The Night of Power is better than a thousand months.

Word by Word (right to left)

لَيْلَةُlaylatuNight of
الْقَدْرِal-qadriThe Power
خَيْرٌkhayrunBetter

Simple Explanation

This is the answer to the question in the previous verse. One thousand months equals approximately 83 years and 4 months. Worship on this single night is better than worshipping for an entire lifetime without it. Ibn Kathir says "better than" means it exceeds 1,000 months, not that it equals them. Mujahid (a student of Ibn Abbas) said this means the good deeds, fasting, and standing in prayer on this night are better than doing so for 1,000 months that do not contain Laylatul Qadr. This is an unimaginable gift from Allah to this Ummah.

4
تَنَزَّلُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ وَالرُّوحُ فِيهَا بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِم مِّن كُلِّ أَمْرٍ
Tanazzalul-mala'ikatu war-ruhu fiha bi-idhni rabbihim min kulli amr
The angels and the Spirit descend therein by permission of their Lord for every matter.

Word by Word (right to left)

Simple Explanation

"Tanazzalu" uses the form that indicates continuous, repeated descending. On this night, the angels descend to the earth in great numbers. "Ar-Ruh" (the Spirit) refers to Jibreel (peace be upon him), who is mentioned separately to honor his high status. Ibn Kathir explains that they descend with goodness, mercy, and blessings. "Min kulli amr" means they come carrying the divine decrees for every matter that will occur in the coming year. As-Sa'di adds that the abundance of angels descending is itself a sign of the immense blessing and mercy of this night.

5
سَلَامٌ هِيَ حَتَّىٰ مَطْلَعِ الْفَجْرِ
Salamun hiya hatta matla'il-fajr
Peace it is until the emergence of dawn.

Word by Word (right to left)

سَلَامٌsalamunPeace

Simple Explanation

The entire night, from Maghrib (sunset) until Fajr (dawn), is completely enveloped in peace (salam). Mujahid said this means that Shaytan cannot do any evil or cause any harm on this night. As-Sa'di explains that "salam" encompasses safety from all harm and evil, and the angels greet the believers with peace. Ibn Kathir mentions that the angels who descend greet every believing man and woman with "salam." The night ends at the break of dawn, marking the completion of this blessed period.

Key Lessons from Surah Al-Qadr
  • ◆
    The Quran is the greatest gift.

    Allah chose the most honorable night of the year to send down His most honorable book. The Quran is not just any book; it is the speech of Allah, and the night it was revealed reflects its immense value.

  • ◆
    One night can change your entire destiny.

    A single night of sincere worship on Laylatul Qadr is worth more than 83 years. This means even a short life dedicated to seeking this night can outweigh decades of worship.

  • ◆
    Seek it in the last ten nights of Ramadan.

    The Prophet (peace be upon him) would intensify his worship in the last ten nights, especially the odd nights (21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, 29th). This is the time to push yourself in prayer, Quran, and du'a.

  • ◆
    Allah's mercy is beyond imagination.

    This night is a gift from Allah to compensate for our short lifespans. He made it easy for us to earn rewards that previous nations with longer lives could not match.

  • ◆
    Peace and tranquility are signs of faith.

    The entire night is described as "salam" (peace). When you worship sincerely, you experience an inner peace that nothing else can provide. This is a taste of what Laylatul Qadr feels like.
