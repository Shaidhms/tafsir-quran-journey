The Story Behind This Surah

Surah Ash-Sharh was revealed during a time of immense difficulty for the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) in Makkah. He faced rejection from his own people, mockery, and the crushing weight of delivering Allah's message to a hostile audience.

This surah is considered a continuation of Surah Ad-Duha (93), where Allah reassured the Prophet that He had not abandoned him. Here, Allah reminds him of specific favors: expanding his chest with faith, removing his burdens, and elevating his status.

The most powerful message comes in verses 5 and 6, repeated for emphasis: "With hardship comes ease." The scholars explain that the word "hardship" (al-'usr) appears with "al" (the definite article), meaning it is ONE specific hardship. But "ease" (yusr) appears without "al", meaning it is indefinite - so there are MULTIPLE eases. Ibn Mas'ud said: "One hardship can never overcome two eases."

Source: Tafsir Ibn Kathir, Tafsir As-Sa'di

The "expansion of the chest" (sharh as-sadr) refers to Allah opening the Prophet's heart to Islam, wisdom, and guidance. Some scholars also reference the physical event where angels opened his chest and washed his heart with Zamzam water as a child and before the Isra' and Mi'raj.

The surah concludes with a profound life principle: when you finish your worldly work, devote yourself to worship. Don't waste your free time. And direct all your hopes and desires toward Allah alone, for He is the ultimate source of relief.

Verse by Verse - With Word-by-Word Arabic

Each verse is broken down word by word. Learn the Arabic gradually by connecting each word to its meaning.

1
أَلَمْ نَشْرَحْ لَكَ صَدْرَكَ
Alam nashrah laka sadrak
Did We not expand for you your breast?

Word by Word (right to left)

أَلَمْalamDid not (We)
نَشْرَحْnashrahWe expand / open
لَكَlakaFor you
صَدْرَكَsadrakYour breast / chest
Simple Explanation

Allah asks a rhetorical question: "Didn't We open your heart wide?" The "expansion of the chest" means Allah filled the Prophet's heart with light, faith, wisdom, and tranquility. When your chest feels "expanded," it means you feel at peace, you can handle difficulties, and your heart is receptive to truth. This is the opposite of a "tight chest" which means anxiety and distress.

2
وَوَضَعْنَا عَنكَ وِزْرَكَ
Wa wada'na 'anka wizrak
And We removed from you your burden.

Word by Word (right to left)

وَوَضَعْنَاwa wada'naAnd We removed / put down
عَنكَ'ankaFrom you
وِزْرَكَwizrakYour burden / load
Simple Explanation

The word "wizr" means a heavy burden or load. This refers to the tremendous weight the Prophet felt from his mission - the responsibility of conveying Allah's message, the pain of seeing his people reject the truth, and the hardships of the early days of Islam. Allah assures him: "We took that weight off you." This is a reminder that Allah does not leave His servants to carry their burdens alone.

3
الَّذِي أَنقَضَ ظَهْرَكَ
Alladhi anqada dhahrak
Which had weighed upon your back.

Word by Word (right to left)

الَّذِيalladhiWhich / That which
أَنقَضَanqadaWeighed down / almost broke
ظَهْرَكَdhahrakYour back
Simple Explanation

The word "anqada" literally means "to make a cracking sound" - like the sound a back makes when it is about to break under an impossibly heavy load. This vivid imagery shows just how heavy the Prophet's burden was. It was so crushing that it nearly broke his back. Yet Allah removed it completely. This teaches us that no matter how overwhelming our difficulties feel, Allah can remove them in an instant.

4
وَرَفَعْنَا لَكَ ذِكْرَكَ
Wa rafa'na laka dhikrak
And We raised high for you your repute.

Word by Word (right to left)

وَرَفَعْنَاwa rafa'naAnd We raised
لَكَlakaFor you
ذِكْرَكَdhikrakYour mention / repute
Simple Explanation

Allah elevated the Prophet's name and mention to the highest degree. Whenever Allah's name is mentioned, Muhammad's name is mentioned right alongside it: in the adhan (call to prayer), in the shahadah (declaration of faith), in the tashahhud during prayer. Mujahid said: "Whenever I (Allah) am mentioned, you (Muhammad) are mentioned with Me." No human being in history has their name mentioned as frequently across the globe as Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

5
فَإِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا
Fa inna ma'al 'usri yusra
For indeed, with hardship comes ease.

Word by Word (right to left)

فَإِنَّfa innaFor indeed / surely
مَعَma'aWith / alongside
الْعُسْرِal-'usriThe hardship
يُسْرًاyusranEase (an ease)
Simple Explanation

This is one of the most comforting verses in the entire Quran. Notice the word "ma'a" - it means "with," not "after." The ease is not waiting for you after the hardship ends. It is RIGHT THERE alongside the hardship. Even in your darkest moment, Allah has already placed ease within reach. The definite article "al" on "al-'usr" (THE hardship) means it is one specific difficulty, while "yusr" (ease) without "al" means it is unlimited and indefinite. Multiple doors of ease open with every single trial.

6
إِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا
Inna ma'al 'usri yusra
Indeed, with hardship comes ease.

Word by Word (right to left)

إِنَّinnaIndeed / surely
مَعَma'aWith / alongside
الْعُسْرِal-'usriThe hardship
يُسْرًاyusranEase (an ease)
Simple Explanation

Allah repeats this promise for absolute emphasis and certainty. In Arabic grammar, when "al-'usr" (the hardship) is repeated with the definite article, it refers to the SAME hardship. But "yusr" (ease) appears without the definite article both times, meaning each mention refers to a DIFFERENT ease. So one hardship brings two eases! This is why Ibn Mas'ud (may Allah be pleased with him) said: "If hardship were to enter a hole, ease would enter after it. One hardship can never overcome two eases."

7
فَإِذَا فَرَغْتَ فَانصَبْ
Fa idha faraghta fansab
So when you have finished [your duties], then stand up [for worship].

Word by Word (right to left)

فَإِذَاfa idhaSo when
فَرَغْتَfaraghtaYou have finished
فَانصَبْfansabThen exert yourself (in worship)
Simple Explanation

When you complete your daily tasks and responsibilities, don't just relax or waste time. Instead, "fansab" - exert yourself in worship and du'a. The word "nasb" means to work hard, to tire oneself out. So even worship requires effort and dedication. Some scholars say this means: when you finish your obligatory prayers, engage in extra voluntary prayers and du'a. Others say: when you finish your worldly work during the day, devote your free time to worshipping Allah.

8
وَإِلَىٰ رَبِّكَ فَارْغَب
Wa ila rabbika farghab
And to your Lord direct [your] longing.

Word by Word (right to left)

وَإِلَىٰwa ilaAnd to / toward
رَبِّكَrabbikaYour Lord
فَارْغَبfarghabDirect your longing / desire
Simple Explanation

The word "raghba" means to earnestly desire and hope. Allah tells us to direct ALL our hopes, desires, and aspirations toward Him alone. Don't place your ultimate hopes in people, wealth, or status. When you need something, ask Allah. When you long for relief, turn to Allah. When you want success, depend on Allah. He is the only One who can truly grant you what you need. This is the ultimate conclusion of the surah: trust Allah completely, and He will take care of everything.

Key Lessons from Surah Ash-Sharh
  • ◆
    Hardship is never permanent.

    Allah promises that ease comes WITH hardship, not just after it. No matter how dark things seem, relief is already built into your trial.

  • ◆
    One hardship, two eases.

    The Arabic grammar proves it: "the hardship" is definite (one), while "ease" is indefinite (multiple). A single difficulty can never overpower the multiple forms of relief Allah sends.

  • ◆
    Allah removes burdens you cannot carry.

    Even the Prophet felt overwhelmed, and Allah lifted his burden. If you feel crushed by life, know that Allah sees your struggle and will lighten your load.

  • ◆
    Don't waste your free time.

    When your daily work is done, turn to worship and du'a. The most productive people are those who use every moment purposefully.

  • ◆
    Direct all your hopes toward Allah.

    People, wealth, and status will all disappoint you at some point. Only Allah never fails those who turn to Him with sincerity.
