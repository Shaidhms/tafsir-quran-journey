The Story Behind This Surah

Surah Al-A'la is one of the earliest surahs revealed in Makkah. It holds a special place because the Prophet (peace be upon him) loved to recite it regularly in Jumu'ah prayers and in both Eid prayers, pairing it with Surah Al-Ghashiyah (88). This wasn't occasional; it was his consistent practice.

An-Nu'man ibn Bashir reported: "The Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) used to recite Surah Al-A'la (87) and Surah Al-Ghashiyah (88) in the two Eid prayers and in the Jumu'ah prayer. If the Eid and Jumu'ah fell on the same day, he would still recite both of them in both prayers."

Source: Sahih Muslim 878

The surah opens with a powerful command: "Sabbih isma Rabbikal-A'la" (Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High). When this verse was revealed, the Prophet (peace be upon him) told his companions: "Place it in your sujood (prostration)." This is the origin of saying "Subhana Rabbiyal-A'la" in sujood, words that every Muslim repeats in every prayer.

The surah then moves through Allah's creative power, how He shaped creation with perfect balance, how He made the earth produce green pasture and then turned it to dark stubble. It contains the remarkable promise that Allah will make the Quran easy for the Prophet to recite and remember, never to forget it. This was a direct comfort during the early, difficult days of revelation.

The final verses connect this message to the scriptures of Ibrahim and Musa (peace be upon them both), showing that the core message of Islam has always been the same: purify yourself, remember your Lord, and prioritize the Hereafter over this world.

Verse by Verse - With Word-by-Word Arabic

Each verse is broken down word by word. Learn the Arabic gradually by connecting each word to its meaning.

1
سَبِّحِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ الْأَعْلَى
Sabbihisma Rabbikal-A'la
Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High.

Word by Word

سَبِّحِsabbihGlorify
اسْمَismaThe name of
رَبِّكَrabbikaYour Lord
الْأَعْلَىal-a'laThe Most High
Simple Explanation

The surah begins with a direct command: glorify Allah by His name, declaring Him above all imperfection. "Al-A'la" means the Most High, above everything in His essence, His attributes, and His dominion. When this verse was revealed, the Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Put it in your sujood." This is why we say "Subhana Rabbiyal-A'la" when prostrating. Every time you put your forehead on the ground, you are living this verse.

2
الَّذِي خَلَقَ فَسَوَّىٰ
Alladhi khalaqa fasawwa
Who created and proportioned.

Word by Word

الَّذِيalladhiWho / The One who
خَلَقَkhalaqaCreated
فَسَوَّىٰfasawwaAnd proportioned
Simple Explanation

Allah didn't just create things randomly. "Sawwa" means He made everything in perfect proportion and balance. Your eyes are perfectly placed. Your heart beats at just the right rhythm. Every creature is designed with precision. As Ibn Kathir explains, Allah created every creature and then perfected its form, giving each one the shape and structure best suited for its purpose.

3
وَالَّذِي قَدَّرَ فَهَدَىٰ
Walladhi qaddara fahada
And who destined and [then] guided.

Word by Word

وَالَّذِيwalladhiAnd the One who
قَدَّرَqaddaraDestined / Decreed
فَهَدَىٰfahadaAnd then guided
Simple Explanation

Allah decreed the measure and nature of everything, and then guided each creation to fulfill its purpose. The bee knows how to build a honeycomb. The baby knows how to nurse. Birds know their migration routes. As As-Sa'di explains, Allah first determined the qadr (decree/measure) of everything, then guided every creature to what it was made for. This applies to both instinctive guidance for animals and spiritual guidance for humans through revelation.

4
وَالَّذِي أَخْرَجَ الْمَرْعَىٰ
Walladhi akharajal-mar'a
And who brings out the pasture.

Word by Word

وَالَّذِيwalladhiAnd the One who
أَخْرَجَakhrajaBrings out / Produces
الْمَرْعَىٰal-mar'aThe pasture / vegetation
Simple Explanation

From dry, barren earth, Allah brings out lush green vegetation. "Mar'a" refers to all types of plants and pasture that the earth produces. This is a sign the Arabs of Makkah would deeply understand, living in a desert environment where rain and greenery were precious. Allah is the One who causes the earth to burst with life, providing sustenance for all creatures.

5
فَجَعَلَهُ غُثَاءً أَحْوَىٰ
Faja'alahu ghutha'an ahwa
And then makes it black stubble.

Word by Word

فَجَعَلَهُfaja'alahuThen He made it
غُثَاءًghutha'anDry debris / stubble
أَحْوَىٰahwaDark / blackish
Simple Explanation

After bringing out lush green plants, Allah then turns them into dry, dark stubble. "Ghutha'" is dried-out plant debris and "ahwa" means dark or blackened. This is a reminder of the cycle of life and death. Everything in this world is temporary. The greenest garden will eventually wither. Ibn Kathir points out that this illustrates how this worldly life, no matter how beautiful, will come to an end, while only what is with Allah will remain.

6
سَنُقْرِئُكَ فَلَا تَنسَىٰ
Sanuqri'uka fala tansa
We will make you recite, and you will not forget.

Word by Word

سَنُقْرِئُكَsanuqri'ukaWe will make you recite
فَلَاfalaAnd you will not
تَنسَىٰtansaForget
Simple Explanation

This is a direct promise from Allah to His Prophet: "We will teach you to recite the Quran, and you will never forget it." The Prophet (peace be upon him) used to anxiously move his lips during revelation, afraid he might forget the words. Allah reassured him here and in Surah Al-Qiyamah (75:16-17) not to worry. The preservation of the Quran in the Prophet's heart was guaranteed by Allah Himself. This promise extends to the entire Ummah, as the Quran remains perfectly preserved to this day.

7
إِلَّا مَا شَاءَ اللَّهُ ۚ إِنَّهُ يَعْلَمُ الْجَهْرَ وَمَا يَخْفَىٰ
Illa ma sha'Allahu innahu ya'lamul-jahra wa ma yakhfa
Except what Allah should will. Indeed, He knows what is declared and what is hidden.

Word by Word

إِلَّاillaExcept
مَاmaWhat
شَاءَsha'aWills
اللَّهُAllahuAllah
إِنَّهُinnahuIndeed He
يَعْلَمُya'lamuKnows
الْجَهْرَal-jahraWhat is open / declared
وَمَاwa maAnd what
يَخْفَىٰyakhfaIs hidden
Simple Explanation

The exception "illa ma sha'Allah" refers to verses that Allah chose to abrogate or cause to be forgotten as part of His divine wisdom. The second part of the verse is a powerful reminder: Allah knows everything you say out loud AND everything you conceal in your heart. Nothing is hidden from Him. As As-Sa'di explains, this should bring both comfort (He knows your struggles even when no one else does) and accountability (He knows your secret intentions too).

8
وَنُيَسِّرُكَ لِلْيُسْرَىٰ
Wa nuyassiruka lil-yusra
And We will ease you toward ease.

Word by Word

وَنُيَسِّرُكَwa nuyassirukaAnd We will ease you
لِلْيُسْرَىٰlil-yusraToward ease
Simple Explanation

Allah promises to make the path easy for the Prophet. "Yusra" comes from the root meaning ease and facilitation. This isn't just about memorizing the Quran. It's about the entire mission: preaching, living righteously, and following the Shariah. Allah made the religion of Islam easy and natural. As Ibn Kathir notes, this means Allah will make all matters of goodness easy for him and facilitate for him the path of righteousness. This applies to all believers too: when you sincerely seek Allah's path, He makes it easier for you.

9
فَذَكِّرْ إِن نَّفَعَتِ الذِّكْرَىٰ
Fadhakkir in nafa'atidh-dhikra
So remind, if the reminder should benefit.

Word by Word

فَذَكِّرْfadhakkirSo remind
إِنinIf
نَّفَعَتِnafa'atiBenefits / is useful
الذِّكْرَىٰadh-dhikraThe reminder
Simple Explanation

Allah tells the Prophet to keep reminding people. The phrase "if the reminder benefits" does not mean "only remind when it works." Scholars explain it means: the reminder ALWAYS benefits someone, so keep giving it. As As-Sa'di points out, this is not a restriction but an encouragement. The reminder benefits the believer, and it serves as proof against the disbeliever. Your job is to deliver the message, not to control who accepts it.

10
سَيَذَّكَّرُ مَن يَخْشَىٰ
Sayadhdhakkaru man yakhsha
He who fears [Allah] will be reminded.

Word by Word

سَيَذَّكَّرُsayadhdhakkaruWill be reminded / will heed
مَنmanThe one who
يَخْشَىٰyakhshaFears (Allah)
Simple Explanation

The one who has khashya (reverent fear and awe of Allah) will take heed and benefit from the reminder. Khashya is not blind terror. It is the deep, informed awareness that Allah sees everything, combined with love and respect for Him. This type of person has a soft heart. When they hear the truth, they accept it. When they hear a warning, they change. The reminder reaches the hearts that are already open.

11
وَيَتَجَنَّبُهَا الْأَشْقَى
Wa yatajannabuhal-ashqa
But the wretched one will avoid it.

Word by Word

وَيَتَجَنَّبُهَاwa yatajannabuhaAnd will avoid it
الْأَشْقَىal-ashqaThe most wretched
Simple Explanation

"Al-Ashqa" is the superlative form, meaning the MOST wretched, the most miserable. This person actively avoids the reminder. They don't just ignore it passively; "yatajannabu" means they deliberately go out of their way to avoid it. They see the truth but choose to turn away. As Ibn Kathir explains, this refers to the disbelievers who stubbornly refused guidance despite clear signs. Their wretchedness is not because Allah didn't send them guidance, but because they chose to reject it.

12
الَّذِي يَصْلَى النَّارَ الْكُبْرَىٰ
Alladhi yaslan-Naral-kubra
Who will [enter and] burn in the greatest Fire.

Word by Word

الَّذِيalladhiThe one who
يَصْلَىyaslaWill burn in
النَّارَan-NaraThe Fire
الْكُبْرَىٰal-kubraThe greatest
Simple Explanation

"An-Nar al-Kubra" (the greatest Fire) refers to the Fire of the Hereafter. Some scholars say "kubra" (greatest) distinguishes it from the fire of this world, emphasizing that the punishment of the Hereafter is incomparably more severe. The wretched person who avoids the reminder will face this consequence. This is not to scare without purpose but to create urgency: the stakes are real. The path of ignoring divine guidance leads to a real and serious end.

13
ثُمَّ لَا يَمُوتُ فِيهَا وَلَا يَحْيَىٰ
Thumma la yamutu fiha wa la yahya
Then he will neither die therein nor live.

Word by Word

ثُمَّthummaThen
لَاlaNot
يَمُوتُyamutuHe will die
فِيهَاfihaIn it
وَلَاwa laAnd not
يَحْيَىٰyahyaWill he live
Simple Explanation

A chilling description: the person in the Fire will neither die (which would end their suffering) nor truly live (which would mean comfort). They exist in a state between the two. Death would be a mercy, but it won't come. As Ibn Kathir explains, they will not have a pleasant life nor will they find relief through death. This is the ultimate consequence for those who persistently rejected guidance and turned away from Allah's reminders.

14
قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَن تَزَكَّىٰ
Qad aflaha man tazakka
He has certainly succeeded who purifies himself.

Word by Word

قَدْqadCertainly / Indeed
أَفْلَحَaflahaHas succeeded
مَنmanThe one who
تَزَكَّىٰtazakkaPurifies himself
Simple Explanation

After the warning comes the beautiful hope. "Qad aflaha" - he has CERTAINLY succeeded. The word "falah" means ultimate, complete success. And the key to this success? "Tazakka" - purifying yourself. This means purifying your soul from shirk (associating partners with Allah), sins, and bad character. It also includes paying zakah (charity), as both come from the same root. As As-Sa'di explains, this purification encompasses cleansing one's beliefs, actions, and wealth. True success is not wealth or status; it is the purity of your soul.

15
وَذَكَرَ اسْمَ رَبِّهِ فَصَلَّىٰ
Wa dhakarasma Rabbihi fasalla
And mentions the name of his Lord and prays.

Word by Word

وَذَكَرَwa dhakaraAnd remembers / mentions
اسْمَismaThe name of
رَبِّهِrabbihiHis Lord
فَصَلَّىٰfasallaAnd then prays
Simple Explanation

The successful person does two things: remembers Allah and prays. "Dhakara" means to remember, mention, and keep Allah in mind throughout the day. "Fasalla" - and then prays. Prayer is the physical manifestation of that remembrance. Notice the beautiful connection back to verse 1: the surah opened with "Glorify the name of your Lord" and now describes the successful person as one who "mentions the name of his Lord and prays." The structure comes full circle. Remembrance of Allah and salah together are the formula for success.

16
بَلْ تُؤْثِرُونَ الْحَيَاةَ الدُّنْيَا
Bal tu'thirunal-hayatad-dunya
But you prefer the worldly life.

Word by Word

بَلْbalBut / Rather
تُؤْثِرُونَtu'thirunYou prefer
الْحَيَاةَal-hayataThe life
الدُّنْيَاad-dunyaOf this world (the lower)
Simple Explanation

A direct, honest diagnosis of the human problem. "Tu'thirun" means to give preference to, to choose over something else. People choose the immediate, visible, tangible pleasures of this world over the unseen rewards of the Hereafter. Note that "dunya" literally means "the lower" or "the nearer." The very name tells you its rank. This is not saying the world is evil, but that making it your priority over the Akhirah is the fundamental mistake that leads people astray.

17
وَالْآخِرَةُ خَيْرٌ وَأَبْقَىٰ
Wal-akhiratu khayrun wa abqa
While the Hereafter is better and more lasting.

Word by Word

وَالْآخِرَةُwal-akhiratuAnd the Hereafter
خَيْرٌkhayrunIs better
وَأَبْقَىٰwa abqaAnd more lasting
Simple Explanation

Two qualities make the Hereafter superior: it is BETTER in quality ("khayr") and MORE LASTING in duration ("abqa"). This world's pleasures are temporary and often mixed with pain. The Hereafter's rewards are pure and eternal. As Ibn Kathir explains, anything in this world, no matter how beautiful, is temporary and will end. But the rewards of the Hereafter are both superior in quality and everlasting. When you truly internalize this, your priorities shift naturally.

18
إِنَّ هَٰذَا لَفِي الصُّحُفِ الْأُولَىٰ

This message is not new. The core teachings of this surah - purifying yourself, remembering Allah, prioritizing the Hereafter - were present in the earlier scriptures too. "Suhuf" refers to the scrolls or pages given to previous prophets. This proves that Islam's message is not an invention but a continuation of the eternal truth that Allah has always sent. Every prophet brought the same essential message: worship Allah alone and prepare for the afterlife.

19
صُحُفِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَمُوسَىٰ
Suhufi Ibrahima wa Musa
The scriptures of Ibrahim (Abraham) and Musa (Moses).

Word by Word

صُحُفِsuhufiThe scriptures of
إِبْرَاهِيمَIbrahimaIbrahim (Abraham)
وَمُوسَىٰwa MusaAnd Musa (Moses)
Simple Explanation

The surah concludes by naming two of the greatest prophets: Ibrahim and Musa (peace be upon them both). Ibrahim received scrolls (suhuf), and Musa received the Torah. The message of purification, prayer, and prioritizing the Hereafter was in their scriptures too. As Ibn Kathir explains, this confirms that Islam is not a new religion but the final, perfected form of the same message given to all prophets throughout history. The chain of guidance from Allah has always been consistent, from Ibrahim to Musa to Muhammad (peace be upon them all).

Key Lessons from Surah Al-A'la
  • ◆
    Glorify Allah in every prostration.

    The command "Sabbih isma Rabbikal-A'la" is why we say "Subhana Rabbiyal-A'la" in sujood. Every prayer is a chance to live this surah. Make those words intentional, not mechanical.

  • ◆
    Allah's creation is designed with perfect purpose.

    Nothing is random. Every creature is proportioned, given its nature, and guided to fulfill its role. If Allah guides the bee to build and the bird to migrate, He can certainly guide your heart to the truth.

  • ◆
    The Quran's preservation is a divine guarantee.

    Allah promised "you will not forget" and He fulfilled it. The Quran has been preserved letter by letter for over 1400 years. No other book in history has this level of protection. Trust the One who made that promise.

  • ◆
    True success is purification of the soul.

    "Qad aflaha man tazakka" - success is defined by the state of your soul, not the state of your bank account. Purify your beliefs, your actions, and your character. That is the real investment.

  • ◆
    The Hereafter is better AND more lasting.

    This world's pleasures are temporary and mixed with hardship. The Hereafter's rewards are pure and eternal. When you truly believe this, your daily choices change. You stop chasing what fades and start building what lasts.

  • ◆
    Islam's message has always been the same.

    From Ibrahim to Musa to Muhammad (peace be upon them all), the core message never changed: worship Allah alone, purify yourself, and prepare for the Hereafter. Islam is not a new invention but the final, perfected form of eternal truth.
