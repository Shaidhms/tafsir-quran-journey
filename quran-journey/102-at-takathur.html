The Story Behind This Surah

At-Takathur means "competition in increase" or "mutual rivalry." It refers to the human tendency to compete with one another in accumulating worldly things: wealth, children, status, possessions, and anything that boosts one's ego.

The Arabs of Makkah would boast about their numbers, their wealth, and even the number of graves belonging to their ancestors. Two clans of the Ansar (Banu Haritha and Banu al-Harith) once boasted about who had more members, even counting their dead in the graveyards to win the argument.

This surah is a wake-up call from Allah. It says: you are so busy competing over worldly things that you have forgotten the purpose of life. You will keep chasing more and more until you end up in your graves. Then you will realize the truth, but by then it will be too late. You will be questioned about every blessing you were given.

Source: Tafsir Ibn Kathir, Tafsir As-Sa'di

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "If the son of Adam had two valleys of gold, he would wish for a third. Nothing fills the belly of the son of Adam except dust (i.e., death)." (Sahih al-Bukhari, Sahih Muslim). This hadith captures the exact spirit of Surah At-Takathur.

Ibn Kathir explains that "takathur" covers all forms of worldly competition: boasting about wealth, children, followers, status, and material possessions. As-Sa'di adds that this distraction prevents people from obeying Allah and preparing for the Hereafter, which is the real loss.

Verse by Verse - With Word-by-Word Arabic

Each verse is broken down word by word. Learn the Arabic gradually by connecting each word to its meaning.

1
أَلْهَاكُمُ التَّكَاثُرُ
Alhakumu at-takathur
Competition in [worldly] increase diverts you.

Word by Word (right to left)

أَلْهَاكُمُalhakumuDiverts you / distracts you
التَّكَاثُرُat-takathurThe mutual competition for more
Simple Explanation

"Alhakum" comes from "lahw" which means distraction or amusement. Allah is saying that the race to accumulate more wealth, more children, more status, and more possessions has diverted people from worshipping Allah and preparing for the Hereafter. "Takathur" means trying to outdo each other in worldly accumulation. Ibn Kathir says this includes boasting about wealth, children, followers, and even ancestors.

2
حَتَّىٰ زُرْتُمُ الْمَقَابِرَ
Hatta zurtumul-maqabir
Until you visit the graveyards.

Word by Word (right to left)

حَتَّىٰhattaUntil
زُرْتُمُzurtumuYou visit
الْمَقَابِرَal-maqabirThe graveyards
Simple Explanation

Allah uses the word "zurtum" (you visited) rather than "you entered" or "you were buried." This is significant because a visit is temporary. As-Sa'di explains that your stay in the grave is just a visit before your final destination (either Paradise or Hellfire). Some scholars say this also refers to people who went to graveyards to count their dead ancestors to boast about having more. Either way, the message is: you will not stop chasing the world until death catches up with you.

3
كَلَّا سَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ
Kalla sawfa ta'lamun
No! You are going to know.

Word by Word (right to left)

كَلَّاkallaNo! / Nay! (strong rebuke)
سَوْفَsawfaSoon / You will
تَعْلَمُونَta'lamunYou will know
Simple Explanation

"Kalla" is one of the strongest words of rebuke in Arabic. It means "No! Stop! Desist!" Allah is firmly telling people to stop their heedless behavior. "Sawfa ta'lamun" means "you will come to know" - this is a warning about what awaits in the grave and the Hereafter. Ibn Kathir says this first warning refers to the punishment of the grave, where the reality of your choices will begin to unfold.

4
ثُمَّ كَلَّا سَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ
Thumma kalla sawfa ta'lamun
Then no! You are going to know.

Word by Word (right to left)

ثُمَّthummaThen / Moreover
كَلَّاkallaNo! (strong rebuke)
سَوْفَsawfaSoon / You will
تَعْلَمُونَta'lamunYou will know
Simple Explanation

The warning is repeated with "thumma" (then/moreover) to emphasize its severity. Repetition in the Quran always carries extra weight. Ibn Kathir says the first warning refers to the torment of the grave, while the second refers to the Day of Judgment. As-Sa'di explains that this repetition is meant to shake the heedless awake: the matter is so serious that Allah warns twice. When someone warns you twice, you know it is not something to take lightly.

5
كَلَّا لَوْ تَعْلَمُونَ عِلْمَ الْيَقِينِ
Kalla law ta'lamuna 'ilmal-yaqin
No! If you only knew with knowledge of certainty...

Word by Word (right to left)

كَلَّاkallaNo! / Nay!
لَوْlawIf
تَعْلَمُونَta'lamunaYou knew
عِلْمَ'ilmaKnowledge of
الْيَقِينِal-yaqinCertainty
Simple Explanation

"Ilm al-yaqin" (knowledge of certainty) is the first level of certainty. Scholars explain three levels: (1) 'Ilm al-yaqin - knowing something through reliable information (like knowing fire burns because you were told), (2) 'Ayn al-yaqin - seeing it yourself (verse 7), (3) Haqq al-yaqin - experiencing it directly. Allah is saying: if you truly understood what lies ahead with even the first level of certainty, you would abandon this worldly competition immediately.

6
لَتَرَوُنَّ الْجَحِيمَ
Latarawunnal-jahim
You will surely see the Hellfire.

Word by Word (right to left)

لَتَرَوُنَّlatarawunnaYou will surely see
الْجَحِيمَal-jahimThe Hellfire / Blazing Fire
Simple Explanation

"Latarawunna" contains a "lam" of oath and a "nun" of emphasis, making this one of the strongest statements of certainty in the Quran. It is not "maybe" or "perhaps" - you WILL see it. "Al-Jahim" is one of the names of Hellfire, referring to its intense, blazing heat. Ibn Kathir explains that everyone will see the Hellfire on the Day of Judgment, as Allah says in another verse: "There is none of you except he will come to it" (Maryam 19:71). The believers will pass over it safely, but the disbelievers will fall in.

7
ثُمَّ لَتَرَوُنَّهَا عَيْنَ الْيَقِينِ
Thumma latarawunnaha 'aynal-yaqin
Then you will surely see it with the eye of certainty.

Word by Word (right to left)

ثُمَّthummaThen
لَتَرَوُنَّهَاlatarawunnahaYou will surely see it
عَيْنَ'aynaEye of / With the eye
الْيَقِينِal-yaqinCertainty
Simple Explanation

"Ayn al-yaqin" means seeing something with your own eyes, which is the second level of certainty. First you heard about it (verse 5), now you will see it yourself. There will be no room for denial. As-Sa'di explains that this repetition emphasizes that seeing the Hellfire is absolutely unavoidable. The first mention is a general statement, the second confirms it will be witnessed directly and personally. At that point, all the worldly things you competed over will seem completely meaningless.

8
ثُمَّ لَتُسْأَلُنَّ يَوْمَئِذٍ عَنِ النَّعِيمِ
Thumma latus'alunna yawma'idhin 'anin-na'im
Then you will surely be asked that Day about pleasure.

Word by Word (right to left)

ثُمَّthummaThen
لَتُسْأَلُنَّlatus'alunnaYou will surely be asked
يَوْمَئِذٍyawma'idhinThat Day
عَنِ'aninAbout
النَّعِيمِan-na'imThe pleasures / blessings
Simple Explanation

"An-Na'im" covers every blessing Allah gave you: your health, your eyesight, your hearing, cold water on a hot day, food when you were hungry, safety when you were afraid, free time, family, and even the air you breathe. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "The first blessing a servant will be asked about on the Day of Judgment is: 'Did We not give you a healthy body and give you cool water to drink?'" (Tirmidhi). Ibn Kathir explains that this question covers two things: (1) Did you show gratitude for these blessings? (2) Did you use them in obedience to Allah or in disobedience?

Key Lessons from Surah At-Takathur
  • ◆
    Worldly competition is a trap.

    The endless race for more wealth, status, and possessions distracts you from the real purpose of life: worshipping Allah and preparing for the Hereafter.

  • ◆
    Death is the ultimate reality check.

    No matter how much you accumulate, you will end up in the graveyard. Nothing you collected will come with you except your deeds.

  • ◆
    Warnings repeated are warnings to take seriously.

    Allah repeats the warning "you will know" twice, showing how urgent and important this message is. When Allah warns twice, we should pay full attention.

  • ◆
    There are levels of certainty.

    'Ilm al-yaqin (hearing about it), 'Ayn al-yaqin (seeing it), and Haqq al-yaqin (experiencing it). The wise person acts on the first level without needing to reach the others.

  • ◆
    Every blessing will be questioned.

    On the Day of Judgment, you will be asked about every single blessing: your health, food, safety, free time, family. Were you grateful? Did you use them to obey Allah?

  • ◆
    Gratitude is the antidote.

    The cure for takathur (competing for more) is shukr (gratitude). When you are grateful for what you have, you stop chasing what you do not need and focus on what truly matters.
