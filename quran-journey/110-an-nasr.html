The Story Behind This Surah

This surah is widely considered the last complete surah revealed to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). It came during or after the Conquest of Makkah in the 10th year of Hijrah.

After 23 years of struggle, persecution, migration, battles, and patience, the mission was complete. Makkah was conquered without bloodshed. People entered Islam in massive waves.

When this surah was revealed, Abdullah ibn Abbas (may Allah be pleased with him) said it was an indication of the approaching death of the Prophet (peace be upon him). Umar ibn al-Khattab agreed with this interpretation.

Source: Sahih al-Bukhari 4970

The surah is essentially Allah telling the Prophet: "Your mission is accomplished. The victory has come. Now prepare for your meeting with Me through praise and seeking forgiveness."

This is deeply moving. Even the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), the best human being ever created, was told to seek forgiveness. If he needed forgiveness, what about us?

Verse by Verse - With Word-by-Word Arabic

Each verse broken down word by word.

1
إِذَا جَاءَ نَصْرُ اللَّهِ وَالْفَتْحُ
Idha jaa'a nasrullahi wal-fath
When the victory of Allah has come and the conquest.

Simple Explanation

"Idha" (when) indicates certainty - this WILL happen. "Nasr" means divine help and victory. "Al-Fath" refers specifically to the Conquest of Makkah - the city that expelled the Prophet and persecuted believers for 13 years. Allah brought the Prophet back as a conqueror, and he entered with humility, his head bowed so low it nearly touched his camel. This was a victory of mercy, not revenge.

2
وَرَأَيْتَ النَّاسَ يَدْخُلُونَ فِي دِينِ اللَّهِ أَفْوَاجًا
Wa ra'aytan-nasa yadkhuluna fi dinillahi afwaja
And you see the people entering into the religion of Allah in multitudes.

Simple Explanation

Before the conquest, people accepted Islam one by one, often at great personal risk. After the conquest, entire tribes entered Islam in waves - "afwaja" (crowds/multitudes). The word choice is powerful: not trickling in, but flooding in. The mission that started with one man in a cave now encompassed all of Arabia.

3
فَسَبِّحْ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّكَ وَاسْتَغْفِرْهُ ۚ إِنَّهُ كَانَ تَوَّابًا
Fasabbih bihamdi rabbika wastaghfirhu innahu kana tawwaba
Then exalt [Him] with praise of your Lord and ask forgiveness of Him. Indeed, He is ever Accepting of repentance.

Simple Explanation

After the ultimate victory, Allah doesn't say "celebrate" or "take credit." He says: glorify Allah and seek forgiveness. "Tawwab" is an intensive form - Allah doesn't just accept repentance, He LOVES to accept it. After the revelation of this surah, the Prophet (peace be upon him) increased his istighfar (seeking forgiveness) significantly, saying "Subhanallahi wa bihamdihi, astaghfirullah wa atubu ilayh" frequently in his final days.

Key Lessons from An-Nasr
  • ◆
    Victory comes from Allah alone.

    No matter how hard you work, ultimate success is from Allah. Attribute it to Him, not yourself.

  • ◆
    After success, increase worship, not ego.

    The greatest achievements should lead to more humility and gratitude, not pride.

  • ◆
    Even the best of creation sought forgiveness.

    If the Prophet needed to ask for forgiveness, we need it infinitely more.

  • ◆
    Every mission has an end.

    This surah reminds us that life is temporary. Prepare for what comes after.
