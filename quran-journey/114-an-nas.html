The Story Behind This Surah

Surah An-Nas is the last surah of the Quran and one of the two "Mu'awwidhatayn" (the two surahs of seeking refuge), paired with Surah Al-Falaq (113).

Both surahs were revealed when the Prophet (peace be upon him) was afflicted by magic done by a man named Labid ibn al-A'sam. The Angel Jibreel came and taught him these surahs as a cure.

The Prophet (peace be upon him) used to seek refuge with Allah from the jinn and the evil eye until the Mu'awwidhatayn were revealed. When they were revealed, he took to them and abandoned everything else.

Source: Sunan at-Tirmidhi 2058, graded Sahih

While Al-Falaq seeks protection from external evils (darkness, magic, envy), An-Nas focuses on the most dangerous evil: the whispering that enters your own mind and heart. This is the evil of waswas (satanic whispers) - the thoughts that make you doubt, despair, sin, and turn away from Allah.

This surah mentions three attributes of Allah (Lord, King, God of mankind) because the enemy being sought refuge from - Shaytan - is so dangerous that you need to invoke Allah's protection with His most powerful attributes.

Verse by Verse - With Word-by-Word Arabic

Each verse broken down word by word.

1
قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ
Qul a'udhu bi rabbin-nas
Say, "I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind."

Word by Word (right to left)

أَعُوذُa'udhuI seek refuge
بِرَبِّbi rabbiin the Lord (of)
النَّاسِan-nasmankind
Simple Explanation

"Rabb" means Lord, Sustainer, Nurturer. He is the One who created you, raised you, and takes care of you at every moment. You seek refuge in the One who knows you better than you know yourself. He is specifically "Rabb of An-Nas" - Lord of ALL people, not just believers. His lordship encompasses every single human being.

2
مَلِكِ النَّاسِ
Malikin-nas
The Sovereign of mankind.

Word by Word

مَلِكِmalikiKing / Sovereign (of)
النَّاسِan-nasmankind
Simple Explanation

"Malik" means King - the One with absolute authority and sovereignty. While "Rabb" nurtures, "Malik" rules and commands. He has the power to protect you because He is the ultimate authority. No king, president, or ruler has power except by His permission. You're seeking protection from the One whose authority nothing can override.

3
إِلَـٰهِ النَّاسِ
Ilahin-nas
The God of mankind.

Word by Word

إِلَـٰهِilahiGod (of)
النَّاسِan-nasmankind
Simple Explanation

"Ilah" means the One worthy of worship, the One hearts turn to in love, fear, and hope. After Rabb (nurturer) and Malik (sovereign), this is the most intimate - He is your God, the One you worship. Three layers of protection: He nurtures you (Rabb), rules over everything (Malik), and is the One you turn to with your heart (Ilah). Against Shaytan, you need all three.

4
مِن شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ
Min sharril-waswasil-khannas
From the evil of the retreating whisperer.

Word by Word

مِنminfrom
شَرِّsharrievil (of)
الْوَسْوَاسِal-waswasthe whisperer
الْخَنَّاسِal-khannasthe retreating one
Simple Explanation

"Al-Waswas" is the one who whispers - Shaytan. "Al-Khannas" means the one who retreats and hides. This is Shaytan's method: he whispers evil thoughts and then retreats. When you remember Allah, he shrinks back. When you forget Allah, he returns to whisper again. He doesn't announce himself - he works in the shadows of your mind, making sins look attractive and good deeds look difficult.

5
الَّذِي يُوَسْوِسُ فِي صُدُورِ النَّاسِ
Alladhi yuwaswisu fi sudurin-nas
Who whispers in the breasts of mankind.

Word by Word

Simple Explanation

"Sudur" literally means chests/breasts, but in Arabic it refers to the seat of emotions and thoughts - the heart. Shaytan doesn't whisper in your ears - he goes directly to your heart. He places doubts, desires, fears, and negativity right at the center of your being. This is why he's so dangerous - his attacks feel like your own thoughts. You might think "I want to do this sin" when actually it's Shaytan planting that desire.

6
مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ
Minal-jinnati wan-nas
From among the jinn and mankind.

Word by Word

مِنَminafrom
الْجِنَّةِal-jinnatithe jinn

Simple Explanation

This final verse reveals that "whisperers" are not only from the jinn (Shaytan and his soldiers) but also from HUMANS. There are people who act like Shaytan - they whisper bad ideas, encourage sin, plant seeds of doubt, and pull you away from what's right. Bad company, toxic friends, people who normalize sin - they are the "shayateen" among humans. This is why the Quran ends with seeking refuge from BOTH types.

Key Lessons from An-Nas
  • ◆
    Your biggest battle is internal.

    Shaytan's whispers target your heart and mind. Awareness is the first defense.

  • ◆
    Remembering Allah makes Shaytan retreat.

    He is "al-khannas" - the one who retreats when you remember Allah. Dhikr is your shield.

  • ◆
    Not all bad influences are supernatural.

    Human "whisperers" are just as dangerous. Choose your company wisely.

  • ◆
    You need Allah's protection at every level.

    As your Rabb, Malik, and Ilah - He protects you as nurturer, ruler, and the One your heart turns to.

  • ◆
    The Quran begins and ends with seeking Allah.

    Al-Fatiha asks for guidance, An-Nas asks for protection. Guidance and protection - that's everything you need.
