The Story Behind This Surah

Surah Az-Zalzalah paints one of the most vivid pictures of the Day of Judgment in the entire Quran. In just 8 short verses, it describes the terrifying earthquake that will mark the end of this world and the beginning of the next.

The earth, which we walk upon every day thinking it is stable, will be violently shaken. It will throw out everything hidden within it: the dead bodies, the treasures, and all the secrets buried inside it.

The most powerful message of this surah comes in the last two verses: even an atom's weight of good or evil will be shown to you on that Day. Nothing is too small to matter. Every deed counts, no matter how tiny it seems.

Source: Tafsir Ibn Kathir, Tafsir As-Sa'di

Ibn Kathir narrates that when this surah was revealed, Abu Bakr (may Allah be pleased with him) wept and said, "This verse will destroy me!" referring to the verse about an atom's weight of evil. The Prophet (peace be upon him) reassured him. This shows how seriously the companions took the weight of every single deed.

As-Sa'di explains that this surah teaches us that the earth itself will be a witness on the Day of Judgment. It will speak and testify about everything that happened on its surface. Every prayer, every act of charity, every sin, every moment of kindness or cruelty, the earth recorded it all.

Verse by Verse - With Word-by-Word Arabic

Each verse is broken down word by word. Learn the Arabic gradually by connecting each word to its meaning.

1
إِذَا زُلْزِلَتِ الْأَرْضُ زِلْزَالَهَا
Idha zulzilat-il ardu zilzalaha
When the earth is shaken with its final earthquake.

Word by Word (right to left)

إِذَاidhaWhen
زُلْزِلَتِzulzilatIs shaken
الْأَرْضُal-arduThe earth
زِلْزَالَهَاzilzalahaIts (final) earthquake
Simple Explanation

The word "zalzalah" means a violent shaking or earthquake. But this is not just any earthquake. The addition of "zilzalaha" (its earthquake) means the ultimate, final earthquake that belongs to the earth itself. It is the earthquake that will end all of creation. Ibn Kathir says this is the earthquake of the Hour (Day of Judgment) that will level every mountain and flatten every structure.

2
وَأَخْرَجَتِ الْأَرْضُ أَثْقَالَهَا
Wa akhrajat-il ardu athqalaha
And the earth discharges its burdens.

Word by Word (right to left)

وَأَخْرَجَتِwa akhrajatAnd throws out
الْأَرْضُal-arduThe earth
أَثْقَالَهَاathqalahaIts burdens / heavy loads
Simple Explanation

"Athqal" means heavy loads or burdens. The earth will throw out everything hidden inside it: the dead who were buried, the treasures that were hoarded, and the secrets that were concealed. Ibn Kathir explains this includes the dead bodies that will be resurrected, and also the treasures of gold and silver. As-Sa'di adds that the earth will expel everything within it as a sign that this world is truly over.

3
وَقَالَ الْإِنسَانُ مَا لَهَا
Wa qal-al insanu ma laha
And man says, "What is wrong with it?"

Word by Word (right to left)

وَقَالَwa qalaAnd says
الْإِنسَانُal-insanuThe human being
مَاmaWhat
لَهَاlahaIs wrong with it
Simple Explanation

When this massive earthquake happens, humans will be in total shock and confusion. They will cry out, "What is happening to the earth?!" This shows the sheer terror and bewilderment of that moment. People had lived their entire lives on this earth thinking it was permanent and stable, but now it is shaking apart beneath them. As-Sa'di notes that this question reflects the utter helplessness of mankind on that Day.

4
يَوْمَئِذٍ تُحَدِّثُ أَخْبَارَهَا
Yawma-idhin tuhaddithu akhbaraha
That Day, it will report its news.

Word by Word (right to left)

يَوْمَئِذٍyawma-idhinThat Day
تُحَدِّثُtuhaddithuIt will report / tell
أَخْبَارَهَاakhbarahaIts news / stories
Simple Explanation

The earth itself will speak on the Day of Judgment! It will testify about everything that happened on its surface. The Prophet (peace be upon him) asked his companions, "Do you know what its news is?" They said, "Allah and His Messenger know best." He said, "Its news is that it will testify about every servant, male and female, about what they did on its surface." (Reported by At-Tirmidhi) Every spot of ground you prayed on, or sinned on, will speak as a witness.

5
بِأَنَّ رَبَّكَ أَوْحَىٰ لَهَا
Bi-anna rabbaka awha laha
Because your Lord has commanded it.

Word by Word (right to left)

بِأَنَّbi-annaBecause
رَبَّكَrabbakaYour Lord
أَوْحَىٰawhaHas inspired / commanded
لَهَاlahaTo it (the earth)
Simple Explanation

Why will the earth speak? Because Allah commanded it to. The word "awha" means to inspire or command. Allah has absolute control over all of creation, even the earth beneath our feet obeys Him. When He commands it to speak, it will speak. As-Sa'di explains that this shows Allah's complete dominion over everything. Nothing in the universe can disobey His command.

6
يَوْمَئِذٍ يَصْدُرُ النَّاسُ أَشْتَاتًا لِّيُرَوْا أَعْمَالَهُمْ
Yawma-idhin yasdur-un nasu ashtatan li-yuraw a'malahum
That Day, people will depart separated to be shown their deeds.

Word by Word (right to left)

يَوْمَئِذٍyawma-idhinThat Day
يَصْدُرُyasduruWill depart / come forth
النَّاسُan-nasuThe people
أَشْتَاتًاashtatanSeparated / in groups
لِّيُرَوْاli-yurawTo be shown
أَعْمَالَهُمْa'malahumTheir deeds
Simple Explanation

On that Day, people will emerge from their graves and go forth in different groups. Some will be heading toward Paradise, others toward the Hellfire. The word "ashtatan" means scattered and separated. Families, friends, nations will all be split apart based on their deeds. The purpose? To be shown exactly what they did in their lives. Ibn Kathir explains that each person will see their complete record, nothing hidden, nothing forgotten.

7
فَمَن يَعْمَلْ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ خَيْرًا يَرَهُ
Faman ya'mal mithqala dharratin khayran yarah
Whoever does an atom's weight of good will see it.

Word by Word (right to left)

فَمَنfamanSo whoever
يَعْمَلْya'malDoes
مِثْقَالَmithqalaWeight of
ذَرَّةٍdharratinAn atom / a speck
خَيْرًاkhayranGood
يَرَهُyarahWill see it
Simple Explanation

This is one of the most famous verses in the Quran. "Dharrah" originally meant the smallest ant or a tiny speck of dust visible in a ray of sunlight. Allah is saying: even the tiniest good deed will be recorded and shown to you. A smile, a kind word, removing a stone from someone's path, giving even half a date in charity. Nothing is too small. Ibn Kathir says this verse is a source of great hope for the believers: no good deed is ever wasted.

8
وَمَن يَعْمَلْ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ شَرًّا يَرَهُ
Wa man ya'mal mithqala dharratin sharran yarah
And whoever does an atom's weight of evil will see it.

Word by Word (right to left)

وَمَنwa manAnd whoever
يَعْمَلْya'malDoes
مِثْقَالَmithqalaWeight of
ذَرَّةٍdharratinAn atom / a speck
شَرًّاsharranEvil
يَرَهُyarahWill see it
Simple Explanation

The mirror image of the previous verse. Just as no good deed is too small to count, no evil deed is too small to be recorded either. A lie, a hurtful glance, a moment of arrogance, backbiting about someone - it all adds up. This verse is a warning: do not dismiss any sin as "small." As-Sa'di explains that this should make a believer cautious about every action, because Allah's accounting is precise and complete. The solution? Repentance (tawbah) can erase sins before that Day comes.

Key Lessons from Surah Az-Zalzalah
  • ◆
    Every deed matters, no matter how small.

    An atom's weight of good or evil will be shown to you. Never belittle a good deed and never dismiss a sin as insignificant.

  • ◆
    The earth is a witness over us.

    Every spot of ground you walk on records what you do. The earth will testify on the Day of Judgment about everything that happened on its surface.

  • ◆
    The Day of Judgment is real and terrifying.

    The earthquake that ends this world will be unlike anything humanity has ever experienced. Preparation for that Day should be our top priority.

  • ◆
    Allah's command is absolute.

    When Allah commands the earth to speak, it speaks. Nothing in creation can disobey Him. This should fill us with awe and motivate us to obey Him.

  • ◆
    People will be separated by their deeds.

    On that Day, no wealth, family name, or status will matter. People will be grouped solely based on what they did in this life.
