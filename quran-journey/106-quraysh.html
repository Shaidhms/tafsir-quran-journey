The Story Behind This Surah

The Quraysh were the most prestigious tribe in Arabia. They were the custodians of the Kaaba and held a special status among all the Arab tribes. Their position gave them safe passage for trade throughout the Arabian Peninsula.

They had two major trade journeys each year: a winter journey to Yemen (for warmth and goods from Africa and India) and a summer journey to Syria (Ash-Sham) for trade with the Byzantine Empire.

This surah is closely connected to Surah Al-Fil (105). Allah destroyed the army of Abraha who tried to destroy the Kaaba. That event increased the Quraysh's prestige and made their trade routes even safer. So Allah reminds them: since I did all this for you, worship Me alone.

Source: Tafsir Ibn Kathir, Tafsir As-Sa'di

Despite all these blessings, many of the Quraysh refused to worship Allah alone and clung to idol worship. This surah is a gentle but firm reminder: everything you have comes from Allah. Your safety, your food, your trade, your prestige. So turn to Him in gratitude.

Some scholars (like Ibn Kathir) consider this surah to be a continuation of Surah Al-Fil, as if saying: "We destroyed the elephant army FOR the sake of the Quraysh's security, so let them worship the Lord of this House."

Verse by Verse - With Word-by-Word Arabic

Each verse is broken down word by word. Learn the Arabic gradually by connecting each word to its meaning.

1
لِإِيلَافِ قُرَيْشٍ
Li-ilafi Quraysh
For the accustomed security of the Quraysh.

Word by Word (right to left)

لِإِيلَافِli-ilafiFor the familiarity / security of
قُرَيْشٍQurayshQuraysh (the tribe)
Simple Explanation

The word "ilaf" means familiarity, security, and being accustomed to something. Allah is pointing to the special arrangement He made for the Quraysh tribe. They were so well-known and respected that they could travel safely across Arabia without being attacked by bandits or rival tribes. This was a huge blessing that no other tribe enjoyed to this extent.

2
إِيلَافِهِمْ رِحْلَةَ الشِّتَاءِ وَالصَّيْفِ
Ilafihim rihlat ash-shita'i was-sayf
Their accustomed security in the caravan of winter and summer.

Word by Word (right to left)

إِيلَافِهِمْilafihimTheir familiarity
رِحْلَةَrihlataJourney / caravan
الشِّتَاءِash-shita'iThe winter
وَالصَّيْفِwas-sayfAnd the summer
Simple Explanation

This verse specifies the blessing: the Quraysh had two major trade caravans each year. In winter, they traveled south to Yemen where it was warmer and they could access goods from Africa, India, and beyond. In summer, they traveled north to Syria (Ash-Sham) for trade with the Byzantine Empire. These trade routes made Makkah wealthy and prosperous, and it was all because Allah gave them safety and prestige as the custodians of the Kaaba.

3
فَلْيَعْبُدُوا رَبَّ هَٰذَا الْبَيْتِ
Fal-ya'budu rabba hadhal-bayt
Let them worship the Lord of this House (the Kaaba).

Word by Word (right to left)

فَلْيَعْبُدُواfal-ya'buduSo let them worship
رَبَّrabbaThe Lord of
هَٰذَاhadhaThis
الْبَيْتِal-baytThe House (Kaaba)
Simple Explanation

"Fa" means "so" or "therefore" - because of all these blessings, here is what you should do: worship the Lord of this House. "This House" refers to the Kaaba in Makkah. Allah did not say "worship the House" but "worship the LORD of this House." The Kaaba itself is not to be worshipped, but its Creator and Sustainer. The Quraysh knew the Kaaba was sacred, but many of them filled it with idols. Allah is telling them to worship Him alone, not the idols.

4
الَّذِي أَطْعَمَهُم مِّن جُوعٍ وَآمَنَهُم مِّنْ خَوْفٍ
Alladhi at'amahum min ju'in wa-amanahum min khawf
Who has fed them against hunger and made them safe from fear.

Word by Word (right to left)

الَّذِيalladhiWho (The One Who)
أَطْعَمَهُمat'amahumFed them
مِّن جُوعٍmin ju'inAgainst hunger
وَآمَنَهُمwa-amanahumAnd made them safe
مِّنْ خَوْفٍmin khawfFrom fear
Simple Explanation

This final verse summarizes the two greatest blessings any human needs: food and safety. Makkah is in a barren valley with no farmland, yet Allah provided for its people through the trade routes. And while the rest of Arabia was plagued by tribal warfare and highway robbery, the Quraysh traveled safely because of their association with the Sacred House. Allah is reminding them (and us): the One who feeds you when you are hungry and protects you when you are afraid deserves your worship and gratitude.

Key Lessons from Surah Quraysh
  • ◆
    Blessings demand gratitude.

    When Allah gives you safety, provision, and status, the correct response is to worship Him, not to become arrogant or forget Him.

  • ◆
    Food and safety are from Allah alone.

    The two most basic human needs - food and security - are gifts from Allah. No amount of wealth or power can guarantee them without His permission.

  • ◆
    Privilege comes with responsibility.

    The Quraysh had a special status among the Arabs. With that privilege came the responsibility to recognize and worship the source of their blessings.

  • ◆
    Reflect on what you take for granted.

    Just as the Quraysh became so accustomed to their blessings that they forgot to be grateful, we too can forget to thank Allah for our daily provisions and safety.
