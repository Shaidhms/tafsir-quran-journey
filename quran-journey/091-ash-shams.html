The Story Behind This Surah

Surah Ash-Shams is one of the most powerful surahs in the Quran in terms of its rhetorical structure. It contains 11 consecutive oaths - more than any other surah. Allah swears by the sun, the moon, the day, the night, the sky, the earth, and the human soul. All of this is to emphasize one single point: the soul and its purification.

The surah then presents the central choice every human faces: to purify the soul through taqwa (God-consciousness) or to corrupt it through sin. Success belongs to those who purify their souls, and failure to those who corrupt them.

The Prophet (peace be upon him) used to recite Surah Ash-Shams in the Isha prayer. When Mu'adh ibn Jabal led the prayer and recited Surah Al-Baqarah (a very long surah), a man left the prayer. When the Prophet heard about this, he said: "Are you putting people to trial, O Mu'adh? Why don't you recite Surah Ash-Shams, or Surah Ad-Duha, or Surah Al-A'la?"

Source: Sahih Bukhari 705, Sahih Muslim 465

The surah concludes with the story of Thamud, the ancient people who rejected Prophet Salih and hamstrung the she-camel that was a miraculous sign from Allah. Their destruction serves as a vivid example of what happens when a people collectively corrupt their souls and deny divine guidance.

The structure is masterful: cosmic oaths (verses 1-6) establish the grandeur of creation, the soul's nature (verses 7-10) reveals the inner battlefield, and the story of Thamud (verses 11-15) shows the real-world consequences of choosing corruption over purification.

Verse by Verse - With Word-by-Word Arabic

Each verse is broken down word by word. Learn the Arabic gradually by connecting each word to its meaning.

1
وَالشَّمْسِ وَضُحَاهَا
Wash-shamsi wa duhaha
By the sun and its brightness.

Word by Word

وَwaBy (oath)
الشَّمْسِash-shamsiThe sun
وَضُحَاهَاwa duhahaAnd its brightness
Simple Explanation

Allah begins with a powerful oath by the sun and its radiant morning light ("duha"). The word "duhaha" refers to the brightness and warmth the sun spreads across the earth. The sun is the most visible, most vital creation we see daily - it gives light, warmth, and life. By starting with this, Allah draws attention to the magnificence of His creation before making His point about the human soul.

2
وَالْقَمَرِ إِذَا تَلَاهَا
Wal-qamari idha talaha
And [by] the moon when it follows it.

Word by Word

وَwaAnd (oath)
الْقَمَرِal-qamariThe moon
إِذَاidhaWhen
تَلَاهَاtalahaIt follows it
Simple Explanation

The moon follows the sun - as the sun sets, the moon rises and takes over illuminating the earth with its reflected, gentle light. "Talaha" means to follow or come after. Ibn Kathir explains that this refers to how the moon follows the sun across the sky, receiving its light from the sun and reflecting it. The relationship between sun and moon is one of perfect divine coordination.

3
وَالنَّهَارِ إِذَا جَلَّاهَا
Wan-nahari idha jallaha
And [by] the day when it displays it (the sun).

Word by Word

وَwaAnd (oath)
النَّهَارِan-nahariThe day
إِذَاidhaWhen
جَلَّاهَاjallahaIt reveals / displays it
Simple Explanation

"Jallaha" means to reveal, uncover, or make manifest. When the day comes, it reveals the sun in its full glory and makes everything visible. As-Sa'di explains that the day unveils the world and allows us to see, work, and function. The day is like clarity and truth - it makes everything visible. This pairs with the next verse about the night covering things.

4
وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَاهَا
Wal-layli idha yaghshaha
And [by] the night when it covers it (the sun).

Word by Word

وَwaAnd (oath)
اللَّيْلِal-layliThe night
إِذَاidhaWhen
يَغْشَاهَاyaghshahaIt covers it
Simple Explanation

"Yaghshaha" means to cover, envelop, or shroud. When the night comes, it covers the sun's light and wraps the world in darkness. Ibn Kathir notes that the night and day are opposites that follow each other in perfect rhythm - neither can overtake the other. This contrast between light and darkness, revelation and concealment, is building toward the main point about the soul's choice between purification and corruption.

5
وَالسَّمَاءِ وَمَا بَنَاهَا
Was-sama'i wa ma banaha
And [by] the sky and He who constructed it.

Word by Word

وَwaAnd (oath)
السَّمَاءِas-sama'iThe sky / heaven
وَمَاwa maAnd He who
بَنَاهَاbanahaBuilt / constructed it
Simple Explanation

Now the oaths move from celestial bodies to the structures of creation themselves. "Banaha" means to build or construct. The sky is described as a grand construction - raised without pillars, perfectly designed, vast and awe-inspiring. The word "ma" here can mean "what" (referring to the act of building) or "who" (referring to the Builder, Allah). Either way, the sky's construction points to the power and precision of its Creator.

6
وَالْأَرْضِ وَمَا طَحَاهَا
Wal-ardi wa ma tahaha
And [by] the earth and He who spread it.

Word by Word

وَwaAnd (oath)
الْأَرْضِal-ardiThe earth
وَمَاwa maAnd He who
طَحَاهَاtahahaSpread it out
Simple Explanation

"Tahaha" means to spread out, flatten, or extend. Allah spread the earth wide so that humans and creatures can live on it, walk on it, and benefit from it. As-Sa'di explains that Allah made the earth stable and spread out with mountains, rivers, paths, and provisions for life. The sky above and the earth below - both are signs of Allah's creative power, and both are sworn by to emphasize what comes next about the soul.

7
وَنَفْسٍ وَمَا سَوَّاهَا
Wa nafsin wa ma sawwaha
And [by] the soul and He who proportioned it.

Word by Word

وَwaAnd (oath)
نَفْسٍnafsinA soul
وَمَاwa maAnd He who
سَوَّاهَاsawwahaProportioned / perfected it
Simple Explanation

Now comes the climax of the oaths - the human soul (nafs). "Sawwaha" means to proportion, perfect, and balance. Allah created the human soul with a natural balance, with the capacity for both good and evil. The soul is mentioned after the sun, moon, day, night, sky, and earth because it is the crown of creation. All of those cosmic signs were building up to this: YOUR soul is the most important thing in creation.

8
فَأَلْهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا
Fa'alhamaha fujuraha wa taqwaha
And He inspired it [with discernment of] its wickedness and its righteousness.

Word by Word

فَأَلْهَمَهَاfa'alhamahaThen He inspired it
فُجُورَهَاfujurahaIts wickedness
وَتَقْوَاهَاwa taqwahaAnd its righteousness
Simple Explanation

"Alhamaha" means He inspired, instilled, or gave innate knowledge. Allah placed within every soul the ability to recognize right from wrong. "Fujur" is wickedness, immorality, and sin. "Taqwa" is God-consciousness, righteousness, and piety. Ibn Kathir explains that Allah showed the soul both paths and gave it the ability to choose. This is the fitrah - the natural disposition. Every person KNOWS deep down what is right and what is wrong. The question is: which path will you choose?

9
قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَن زَكَّاهَا
Qad aflaha man zakkaha
He has succeeded who purifies it.

Word by Word

قَدْqadCertainly / indeed
أَفْلَحَaflahaHas succeeded
مَنmanThe one who
زَكَّاهَاzakkahaPurifies it
Simple Explanation

THIS is the answer to all 11 oaths. "Qad aflaha" - truly, certainly successful is the one who purifies their soul. "Zakkaha" comes from tazkiyah, which means to purify, cleanse, and grow. After swearing by the sun, moon, day, night, sky, earth, and soul, the entire point is this: purify your soul. The Prophet (peace be upon him) used to make dua: "O Allah, give my soul its taqwa and purify it, for You are the best to purify it. You are its Guardian and Master." (Sahih Muslim 2722)

10
وَقَدْ خَابَ مَن دَسَّاهَا
Wa qad khaba man dassaha
And he has failed who instills it [with corruption].

Word by Word

وَقَدْwa qadAnd certainly
خَابَkhabaHas failed
مَنmanThe one who
دَسَّاهَاdassahaCorrupts / buries it
Simple Explanation

"Khaba" means to fail, lose, and be disappointed. "Dassaha" is a powerful word - it means to bury, hide, and corrupt. It comes from the root meaning to conceal something in the ground. When someone corrupts their soul, they are essentially burying their own potential, hiding their goodness under layers of sin. As-Sa'di explains that this person diminishes their soul through disobedience until it becomes small and insignificant, when it was meant to be noble and elevated.

11
كَذَّبَتْ ثَمُودُ بِطَغْوَاهَا
Kadhdhabat Thamudu bitaghwaha
Thamud denied [their prophet] by reason of their transgression.

Word by Word

كَذَّبَتْkadhdhabatDenied / rejected
ثَمُودُThamuduThamud (the people)
بِطَغْوَاهَاbitaghwahaBecause of their transgression
Simple Explanation

Now comes the real-world example: the people of Thamud. They were an ancient Arabian tribe who lived in the rock-carved city of Al-Hijr (also called Mada'in Salih). "Kadhdhabat" means they denied their prophet, Salih. And WHY did they deny him? "Bitaghwaha" - because of their transgression and rebellion. Their sin was not ignorance. It was arrogance. They CHOSE to corrupt their souls, and this is what it looked like in practice.

12
إِذِ انبَعَثَ أَشْقَاهَا
Idhin ba'atha ashqaha
When the most wretched of them was sent forth.

Word by Word

إِذِidhiWhen
انبَعَثَinba'athaRose up / was sent forth
أَشْقَاهَاashqahaThe most wretched of them
Simple Explanation

"Ashqaha" means the most wretched, the most miserable. This refers to the man who volunteered to hamstring the she-camel. Ibn Kathir identifies him as Qudar ibn Salif, who was the most wicked person among the Thamud. He rose up eagerly to commit the crime. The word "inba'atha" suggests he was driven forward by his own corruption - his soul, buried under sin, propelled him toward destruction.

13
فَقَالَ لَهُمْ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ نَاقَةَ اللَّهِ وَسُقْيَاهَا
Faqala lahum Rasulullahi naqatallahi wa suqyaha
And the messenger of Allah said to them, "[Do not harm] the she-camel of Allah or [prevent] her from drinking."

Word by Word

فَقَالَfaqalaThen said
لَهُمْlahumTo them
رَسُولُ اللَّهِRasulullahiThe Messenger of Allah
نَاقَةَ اللَّهِnaqatallahiThe she-camel of Allah
وَسُقْيَاهَاwa suqyahaAnd her drink
Simple Explanation

Prophet Salih (peace be upon him) warned them clearly: "This is the she-camel of Allah" - meaning it was a miraculous sign from Allah, not an ordinary animal. "Wa suqyaha" - and leave her share of water alone. The she-camel had a designated day to drink from the well, and the people had their own day. This was a simple test of obedience. The warning was clear, the command was easy. But the corrupted soul finds even simple obedience impossible.

14
فَكَذَّبُوهُ فَعَقَرُوهَا
Fakadhdhabuhu fa'aqaruha
But they denied him and hamstrung her.

Word by Word

فَكَذَّبُوهُfakadhdhabuhuBut they denied him
فَعَقَرُوهَاfa'aqaruhaAnd they hamstrung her
Simple Explanation

"Kadhdhabuhu" - they denied him (Salih). "Aqaruha" - they hamstrung her (the she-camel). Notice that even though one man did the physical act, the verb is plural ("they hamstrung"). Ibn Kathir explains this is because they ALL approved of it and were complicit. When a society collectively agrees to sin and no one speaks up, they ALL share the blame. This is a powerful lesson about collective responsibility and the danger of silent approval of wrongdoing.

15
فَدَمْدَمَ عَلَيْهِمْ رَبُّهُم بِذَنبِهِمْ فَسَوَّاهَا
Fadamdama 'alayhim Rabbuhum bidhanbihim fasawwaha
So their Lord brought down upon them destruction for their sin and made it equal [upon all of them].

Word by Word

فَدَمْدَمَfadamdamaSo He destroyed
عَلَيْهِمْ'alayhimUpon them
رَبُّهُمRabbuhumTheir Lord
بِذَنبِهِمْbidhanbihimFor their sin
فَسَوَّاهَاfasawwahaAnd leveled it equally
Simple Explanation

"Damdama" is an Arabic word that carries the sound of destruction - it means to crush, flatten, and destroy completely. "Fasawwaha" - He made the destruction equal upon all of them. No one was spared, because the sin was collective. As-Sa'di notes that when Allah sent the earthquake and the thunderous scream (as-sayhah), it leveled their homes and lives completely. The verse ends with a profound statement: "wa la yakhāfu 'uqbāhā" - and He does not fear any consequence. Allah's justice is absolute. This is the ultimate warning about what happens when souls are collectively corrupted.

Key Lessons from Surah Ash-Shams
  • ◆
    Your soul is the most important thing in creation.

    Allah swore by the sun, moon, day, night, sky, and earth - all to make one point: purify your soul. The entire cosmos was mentioned to show how important YOUR inner state is.

  • ◆
    Every person knows right from wrong.

    Allah placed within every soul the knowledge of fujur (wickedness) and taqwa (righteousness). No one can claim total ignorance. The fitrah is real, and we are accountable for our choices.

  • ◆
    Success is defined by soul purification, not worldly gain.

    The word "aflaha" (succeeded) is used only for the one who purifies their soul. Not the wealthy, not the powerful. Thamud were wealthy and powerful, and they were destroyed.

  • ◆
    Collective sin brings collective punishment.

    One man hamstrung the camel, but ALL of Thamud were punished because they approved and were complicit. Silence in the face of evil makes you part of it.

  • ◆
    Corruption buries your potential.

    The word "dassaha" (corrupted) literally means to bury. When you choose sin over righteousness, you are burying the best version of yourself under layers of darkness.
